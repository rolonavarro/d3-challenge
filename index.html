States and the corralation with poverty and healthcare

There is a strong correlation between poverty and healthcare. As we can see from this graph as poverty starts to increase, the lack of healthcare also starts to increase. At one end of the spectrum, we have Massachusetts that around 11% of the population and less than 5% of the population lack healthcare. Then we have Mississippi that around 22% of the population live in poverty and almost 20% of the population do not have access to healthcare.
